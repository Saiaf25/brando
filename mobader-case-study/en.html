Chapter 1 · August 2024

Before we started, Mobader's website couldn't be found on Google.

The diagnosis
Chapter 1 The diagnosis
August 2024

Spending on ads with no way to measure them. A site Google couldn't find. The infrastructure was not there.

In August 2024, before any contract was signed, Brandology audited Mobader Home Care's full digital state. The audit was direct.

On the website: 87.5% of pages were missing the basic headings Google looks for. The home page carried a single uncompressed image weighing 33 megabytes. There was no schema, no canonical tags, no security headers. The site couldn't rank on Google for any meaningful query.

On the ads side: Mobader was already running Google Ads, and already spending real money on them. But the landing pages were poor. No call-to-action above the fold. No trust signals. No clear description of what the services were or why a Saudi family should book one. The site speed killed the few visitors the ads did send. Conversion tracking was broken. The team could not measure which campaign produced which lead, which lead produced which booking, or what the ROAS on any campaign actually was. They were spending. They were not measuring. The ROAS was poor and there was no way to prove or improve it.

On the operational side: The mobile app had 10 downloads. The Facebook page had 362 followers. There was no email marketing, no referral system, no automation, no CRM workflow. No way for the sales team to know which calls came from which channel.

The audit's verdict was one sentence: the site cannot rank in its current condition, the ad spend cannot be optimised without measurement, and the marketing infrastructure does not exist. Recommendation: full rebuild.

The build
Chapter 2 The build
December 2024

Three jobs, in order. No magic.

Most agencies start with the loudest deliverable: a new logo, a new ad campaign, a fresh batch of social posts. We started with the quietest one. We installed Google Tag Manager and Google Analytics 4 on Mobader's site, hooked every conversion event into a measurable goal, and routed every form submission directly to the sales team's phones in real-time. None of this is glamorous. It's also the reason every number in this case study is defensible.

Then the website rebuild. WordPress on basic hosting (the platform isn't the bottleneck. Discipline is.). Schema markup across every page so Google could read the site properly. Every technical issue from the audit closed.

Then the content. In-depth medical articles a month, every month, for 11 months. Not marketing fluff, real medical content about cupping, sciatica, Alzheimer's care, post-chemo support. The kind of articles a Saudi family searches for at 2am when they're worried about a parent.

Then, and only then, paid ads. Twenty thousand SAR a month on Google Ads. Adjusted daily. Watched hourly.

  2. 2Rebuilt the website on WordPress.Schema, technical SEO, fast hosting. Google could finally find Mobader.
  3. 3Started publishing medical articles every month.Real content for real medical questions Saudi families search for.
  4. 4Launched Google Ads at 20,000 SAR/month.Daily optimisation. Hourly watch. Spend bumps only when the data said yes.

For the first ten weeks, the organic search chart stayed flat. The paid ads were already producing revenue from day one, but the free-traffic engine takes longer to build. That's the part most agencies hide when they sell SEO.

Chapter 3 · February 2025 onward

In month three, Google started sending real customers. For free.

The compound
Chapter 3 The compound
Feb 2025+

From 50 visits a month to 22,000. Free.

In late February 2025, after ten weeks of weekly publishing, the chart broke its silence. Daily visitors from Google search jumped past 100 for the first time. By March, position on Google had moved from page three to the top of page one. From that month forward, Mobader's organic traffic compounded month after month.

Each new article added another way for a Saudi family to find Mobader. Older articles continued climbing as the site's authority grew. By October 2025, Mobader was earning roughly 22,000 free Google visitors per month (around 720 a day), across cupping queries, sciatica queries, Alzheimer's-care queries, post-chemo support queries. The kind of traffic you can't buy: visitors who arrive trusting you because Google sent them, not because an ad interrupted them.

Chapter 4 · July → November 2025

We doubled the ad spend. Mobader's revenue grew five times.

The peak
Chapter 4 The peak
Jul – Nov 2025

Phase 1 ROAS: 11.5×. Phase 2 ROAS: 10×, sustained for five months.

ROAS means Return on Ad Spend: how many riyals a campaign earned back for every 1 riyal it spent. It is the single number a business owner should ask for when judging any ad campaign. With proper tracking now in place, every Mobader campaign had a measurable ROAS for the first time.

Phase 1 (April 2025). Mobader spent 31,991 SAR on Google Ads that month and earned 369,000 SAR in sales from those campaigns. ROAS: 11.5× (every 1 SAR spent returned 11.5 SAR in revenue). Ninety conversions. Five hundred marketing-qualified leads. The model worked. The next question was how high it could go.

By mid-2025, Mobader had three things working at once: a website Google could find, a content library that earned visitors for free, and a tracking setup that proved every paid lead's journey from click to call to booking. The data was clear. The engine could carry more weight.

Phase 2 (July → November 2025). We raised the Google Ads spend to 50,000 SAR/month. Monthly revenue settled at approximately 500,000 SAR/month, roughly five times Mobader's pre-engagement baseline of about 100,000 SAR/month. ROAS: ~10×, held for five consecutive months. Slightly lower efficiency than Phase 1, much higher absolute revenue.

For context: any Google Ads campaign with a sustained ROAS above 4× is generally considered strong. 10× sustained for five months is exceptional. 11.5× in a single high-efficiency month is the kind of result that proves a model is genuinely working, not just lucky.

For the business owner reading this

If you want this for your business, three honest answers.

1. The math only works if the measurement comes first. Every defensible number above exists because we set up tracking before any spend. If your agency wants to start by running ads "to see what happens," ask them how they will measure what happened.

2. Content compounds. There is no shortcut. Mobader's content library took 11 months of weekly publishing to become the engine that earns roughly 20,000 free Google visitors a month (around 700 a day). Anyone who promises this in 30 days is selling you something else.

3. Plan your sales team for the inbound you're about to generate. A well-built marketing engine can grow your inbound lead volume faster than your sales team can grow to close it. Build the team alongside the engine. The math compounds when both grow together.

Footnote. After the Phase 2 plateau, we ran a deliberate test by raising spend to 100,000 SAR/month. Lead volume doubled within two weeks, confirming the marketing engine's headroom. That headroom is now waiting for the sales-side capacity to grow into it. The 5× revenue growth and the 11.5× Phase 1 ROAS above remain the published, verified results of the engagement.
