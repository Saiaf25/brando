Reading the quarter

Efficiency is trending the right way. After the February dip, cost per lead dropped four periods running, from EGP 674 in March to EGP 416 in June. The late-May burst (EGP 280) came on a small EGP 8,968 test budget, so treat it as directional rather than a stable benchmark.

Volume is recovering with less money. June produced 74 leads on EGP 30,801, roughly half of January's spend for double the leads. Qualification and closing only entered the tracker in the last two periods, so 36 qualified and 4 done are an early read, not a full-funnel verdict.

Next: hold June's targeting, scale the budget back toward the EGP 50–65k range that historically drives volume, and keep qualified and done logged from period one so conversion rates become trustworthy.
